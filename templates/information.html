ANGINA

Angina is chest pain or discomfort resulting from reduced blood flow to the heart muscle, often caused by coronary artery disease.

SYMPTOMS

  1. The main symptom of angina is chest pain or discomfort, often described as pressure, squeezing, burning, or tightness.
  2. The pain may radiate to the shoulders, arms, neck, jaw, or back. Additional symptoms may include shortness of breath, nausea, fatigue, dizziness, and sweating.

    3. ST DEPPRESSION

    ST depression is significant if it is 1 mm (0.1 mV) or more below the baseline in horizontal or downsloping patterns, persisting for at least 0.08 seconds. Upsloping ST depression is considered significant if it is 1.5 mm (0.15 mV) or more.
